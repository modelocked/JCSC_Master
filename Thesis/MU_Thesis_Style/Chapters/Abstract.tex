Place your abstract text here.] Cumulus clouds are puffy, rounded masses that drift across the sky on fair-weather days, resembling heaps of cotton wool or floating cauliflower. They form when warm air rises, cools, and its moisture condenses into visible droplets. Their flat bases sit at a fairly uniform height where condensation begins, while their tops billow upward in bright white domes. Lit by the sun, they appear brilliant against blue sky, with shadowed undersides in grey. Most stay small and harmless, but when they grow tall and dense they can build into towering cumulonimbus storm clouds, bringing rain, lightning, and gusty winds.\par
